## Quick Facts


Enchanting happens by connecting the power of a entity's magic with magical runes upon an object. Linking an entity's magic usually results in the entity's death. Depending on the entity's magical strength and the power requirements of the enchantment, the active time of the enchantment will vary. An enchantment can take multiple entities as a power source and can be renewed before expiry. 


## Detailed Explanation


### Rune Crafting

In order to communitcate to the force that is magic, the enchanter needs to imprint the correct magical formula onto the item in question. The more durable the imprint is the higher the chance for the enchantment to remain stable even after heavy use. This makes stone or metal engravings especially durable compared to writing or weaving.  
If the imprint were to fade off or be somehow removed then the enchantment's magical energy will dissipate, or when the magical charge is still very strong it may be released forcibly. Such a strong release is likely to be harmful to anything in range. 



### Essence Powering


Almost every entity in the Universe of Tem-Bel has an inherent connection to the magical plane. This connection may be stronger or weaker depending on species and genetics, but it is this connection which allows a mage to power their spells, or an enchanter to power their enchantments. Depending on an entity's connections's strength the enchantment might require multiple entities' power to be created as well as to be powered for any amount of time.  
Usually an enchantment's power drain is linear and dependant on the nature of the magic used. If the enchantment loses power but should remain active, the user will have to find a suitable entity to funnel their power into the enchantment so that the enchantment can continue to hold. While this funnelling process does not require precise knowledge of the magic behind the enchantment, it does require knowledge on how to power an enchantment.
