## Quick Facts

Similarly to their larger brethren, the [Dragonborn](Dragonborn.md), Kobolds are a cold blooded, lizard like people. Ranging between 1.2-1.5 metres in height and weighing around 40kg they are less known for their raw physical power and instead for their clever tactics and unusually high inate magical connection. In their home reagion of [Ma'antur](../../Continents/Ma'antur.md) they usually occupy the religious and political roles in society. 


## Home Region

In their home region of [Ma'antur](../../Continents/Ma'antur.md) the Kobold people feel right at home year round. Due to their cold blooded nature the harsh, burning sun is the driving force behind their survival. Since they require less sustenance compared to the warm blooded species, their home region's scalding summers are easier to survive.

## Language

Because of the high frequency of draconic visitors, the Kobold language has not deviated much from the Draconic language, spoken by most of the dragon populace, which first gave them the gift of speech. Instead the Kobold language (officially known as Standard Turian) is more of a dialect that often times lags behind the official draconic language evolution, instead frequently retaining archaisms too amusing for most dragons to point out. This has caused the Kobold language to become almost its own language over the millenia if it were not for the odd dragon being annoyed at the Kobolds weird patterns of speech and educating them about their usage of outdated grammar or vocabulary. Phonetically most Kobolds or Dragonborn are unable to properly articulate many of the phonemes found in proper draconic which usually either amuse or aggravate dragons but has caused the Kobold language to deviate strongly from its written to spoken form. In essence, the written form is phonetically correct draconic which will be pronounced in a Kobold pronounceable manner, making it very much possible that the same text might sound quite different depending on whether a dragon or a Kobold reads it aloud. 


## Religion

They usually follow the belief in the [Great Dragons](../../Religions/Ma'antur/The%20Great%20Dragons.md), native to their [home region](#home-region). While Kobold kind is generally split between Monolism and Dualism it is rare for a Kobold to follow another kind of belief deviating from the core Great Dragon doctrine. This adherence is strongly connected to their draconic features and the relative frequency of draconic visitors the lands of [Ma'antur](../../Continents/Ma'antur.md) receive. 


## Physiology

The small almost emaciated stature that all Kobolds exhibit, paired with their inability to produce their own heat, makes them an incredibly energy efficient life form. This paired with their incredibly heat resistant skin, makes it almost impossible under normal circumstances to induce heat stroke in a Kobold. While a Kobold is definitely not fire proof, trying to boil a Kobold in a super heated room will be an arduous endeavor. The average Kobold will reach about 1.4 metres in height and 40kg in weight. They have small but very sharp teeth, specialized for cutting. Kobolds are omnivorous and equipped to eat almost all kinds of flora and fauna. With their teeth specialized for cutting instead of ripping or crushing, they usually prefer eating bones, green shrubs or similar sustenance. Their vaguely draconic bodies have shed the hard scales which still adorn the bodies of the [Dragonborn](Dragonborn.md) and have fused instead together to form a malleable and durable type of skin. They still have three fingers but instead of long sharp claws the nails at the end are shortened and relatively dull. 


## Cuisine

One might imagine the Turian cuisine to be bland due to the relative scarcity of plant and wildlife in the long summer months, instead Turian cuisine thrives on low ingredient meals. Turian chefs are experts in making flavourful meals without the availability of spices or herbs. Instead they employ different techniques allowing for even the most sun bleached weed to maximize its flavour profile. This makes Turian cooking styles particularly popular among the lower social strata of any country lucky enough to have extened cultural exchange with them.

## Name Examples
