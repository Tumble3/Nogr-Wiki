## Quick Facts

The Orcs hailing from the rumbling volcanic peaks of [Adunica](../../Continents/Adunica.md) are hardy in nature and almost non-senescent, due to their constantly regrowing bodies. Stemming from an intensely nutrient rich volcanic island chain their ancestral roots stem from plants, which had to evolve movement due to seismic activity as much as flooding and cave ins, threatening its survival. While they are often times compared to cold blooded life forms by amateur scientists, their sap like fluid pathways mimicking veins do not stop flowing when they travel through cold environments. Instead an Orc that is cut off from heat will stop growing first, then in the next stage they will start getting lethargic before ultimately going into hibernation until defrosted. Orcs through the abundance of minerals and metals have developed to be a very high technology society, living in tight nit groups around their immediate progenitors. They make excellent use of any and all materials they can find and were the first to develop steam technology. Due to their plant based heritage they are able to harness a more primal type of magic, which relies as much on feeling as on science based information. 


## Home Region

[Adunica](../../Continents/Adunica.md) is the home land of the Orcs. A harsh and violent volcanic line of constantly changing geologic make up. It is from whence the Orcish knack for technology regarding metallurgy comes, since the constantly shifting techtonics shower Orcish communities with resources, making even rare metals be relatively abundant. 


## Culture

Since a new Orc can only come from the cut of parts of an existing Orc, Orcish culture tends to be closely centrered around small tight nit groups sharing a common progenitor. This makes Orc society also highly stratified along the so called "progenitor lines", where one Orc from one of the predominant genetic lineages will be followed by their offspring and seen as the predominant person to look for for advice. Since the genetic differences between an Orc progenitor and its offspring are miniscule and more akin to an imperfect clone, the direct descendants of an Orc tend to be extremely similar to their parent, making Orcish culture extremely slow to change and highly dependant on lineage in question. While each Adunican believes strongly in the inherent power of metal, the exact length the indidivual Orc will go when believing in its power depends highly on the Orc's lineage. Where some lineages will put greater stock in certain metals over others or will generally believe more or less strongly in the power of metals as a whole. 


### Adunican Lineages

#### Todric Lineage

The Todric lineage is named after its oldest known progenitor Todric. They can be easily identified by their purplish green skin colour. They are known for being a lineage more aligned to the primal aspects of magic usage, often referred to by outsiders as the esoteric or shamanistic Orc lineage. While all Adunicans value metals highly, the Todric Adunicans believe that each metal has inherent magical properties given to it by the gods themselves, which can be harnessed if atuned to them properly. They can usually be identified by their extravagant usage of practical metal jewellery, consisting of as many different metals as possible. They adorn every inch of their bodies with little metal plates, either fusing them directly into their skin or letting it hang like wind catcher-esque pendants from their extremeties. One can almost never be surprised by the arrival of an Adunican from the Todric lineage, since their metal adornments jingle incessantly, unless they decide to muffle their sound magically. 

#### Brennan Lineage

Opposite the Todric lineage the Brennan Lineage puts little stock in the power of individual metals, beyond what they can get out of from metallurgical procedures alone. Where the Todric Adunicans derive their magical prowess from their belief in metals, the Brennan Lineage focuses around harnessing magic through scientific testing and application. They will still use metal as a conductor to the gods' will, but instead interpret the resulting resonance through repetition and reproducability. They can be easily identified by the reddish green colouration of their skin. While they too adorn themselves with metal, they use tried and true positioning and placement to optimize the amount of metal for power procurement. If a member of the Brennan lineage ventures out and interacts with other sentient species they can often be found wearing tight almost military like garments with interwoven metal plates allowing direct metal to skin contact at specifically tailoured pressure points. 


#### Amagundan Lineage

The Amagundan Lineage sports a yellow and green skin colour. Their lineage is prone to spawning curious and innovative offspring, greatly advancing Adunican science and exploration efforts. While many of the other lineages provide scientists too, the rather meagre magical aptitude found among Amagundan Orcs leads them in particular to seek other methods of technology that is less reliant on magic. They are also the Adunicans sporting the least amount of metal adornments. Usually they only wear minimal metal to keep their connection to the gods, in the form of a small tiara, a chain around their necks and upper arms. If they choose to wear clothing they choose loosely cut but lose flowing robes, shirts, pants or skirts.

### Reproductive Cycle

If a fully grown Orc cuts of a part of themselves and gives that part enough warmth, it will grow into a new Orc over the course of at least a year in perfect conditions or up to a century in minimal growing conditions.
